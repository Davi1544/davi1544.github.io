## Studies

### Topic: t-SNE and PCA

t-SNE (t-Distributed Stochastic Neighbor Embedding) is a technique used for dimensionality reduction.

As previously stated, a friend working on a similar project suggested this topic to me, because this technique might prove useful in grouping multispectral pixels. 

PCA (Principal Component Analysis) is also a technique for dimensionality reduction. In constrast with t-SNE, which is non-deterministic and works well with non-linear data, PCA is deterministic and works best when the original dimensions of the inputs have linear relationships.

While both t-SNE and PCA have similar purposes, I consider t-SNE to be the best out of the two for the purposes for this project. Despite t-SNE slower execution time, it'll most likely perform better than PCA because it produces more separated clusters. PCA is designed to preserve global structures while t-SNE is designed to preserve local groups, which is extremely useful when our goal is to find pixel patches that might contain hidden text.

## Experiments
To test t-SNE versus PCA, I used the [Kaggle Titanic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hobbit#Lifestyle) dataset. By reducing the number of dimensions from 8 to 2, my goal was to plot the final results and check if either algorithm managed to preserve survivors and non-survivors in separate groups.

As expected, t-SNE had a much better result, managing to keep both groups relatively separate.

![t-SNE Results]({{ site.baseurl }}/assets/week1-TSNE-results.png)

![PCA Results]({{ site.baseurl }}/assets/week1-PCA-results.png)

Observe that t-SNE managed to group most of the survivors in a compressed section, while PCA spread survivors and non-survivors across all major groupings.
